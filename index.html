Ancash Quechua · Peru

Evidential transference in Quechua–Spanish bilinguals

Quechua obligatorily marks how a speaker came to know what they are asserting — witnessed -mi, reported -shi, inferred -chi. Contact with Spanish is expected to erode that system. Comparing a bilingual speaker's retelling of the Achiquee folktale against 1970s Ancash recordings, I argue the evidential semantics survive the loss of the overt suffix: the distinction is still made, just no longer on the verb.

LCUGA 2025 · University of Georgia

Without evidential

Achiquee unay-n tiempos ka-shqan.
‘The Achiquee existed in old times.’

With evidential

…chunki-sh … ka-n-mi.
‘…ten times, they say… it is (I saw).’

Same speaker, same story. The reportative -sh and direct -mi appear in one telling and not the other.

Chechen · Nakh-Daghestanian

Dynamic verbs and scalar change

Vendler's aspectual classes strain against verbs that encode a scale — widen, cool, warm are telic or atelic depending on how far along the scale you look. I adapt Rappaport Hovav's (2008) diagnostics for Chechen, where scalar predicates are built compositionally (mela 'warm' + della 'become'), and test them in grammaticality judgments with a native speaker from Grozny.

Tarifit Berber · Morocco

Rising intonation in polar and wh-questions

Tarifit can mark a yes/no question with the particle [ma] — or drop it and let pitch do the work. Field elicitation and spectrographic analysis of both question types, testing whether rising intonation is the redundant partner of the particle or the thing actually carrying the question. Co-authored fieldwork.

Field methods project · with J. McLendon, M. McNamara, C. Augustine

About

I hold an MA in Spanish Linguistics from the University of Georgia (2025) and a BA in Spanish and Political Science from Emory (2019). At UGA I was an instructor of record in Spanish and a member of the (Se)Mantis Lab research group.

My work runs from elicitation and glossing to quantitative modeling of variation, and I am moving toward doctoral work on the semantic features of evidential systems — how morphology like evidential marking is represented and produced. Alongside research I have spent five years as an immigration paralegal, drafting evidence-heavy EB-1A and National Interest Waiver petitions.

Member: Linguistic Society of America · IEEE.
