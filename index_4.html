About

I hold an MA in Spanish Linguistics from the University of Georgia (2025) and a BA in Spanish and Political Science from Emory (2019). At UGA I was an instructor of record in Spanish and a member of the (Se)Mantis Lab research group.

My work focuses on semantic features of underdocumented languages, and I am moving toward doctoral work studying semantic features of evidential systems. Alongside research I have spent five years as an immigration paralegal, drafting evidence-heavy EB-1A and National Interest Waiver petitions.

Research

Ancash Quechua · Peru

Evidential Transference in Quechua–Spanish Bilingual Speakers

Evidentials in Quechua assert information source (Aikenvald: 2012) witnessed -mi, reported -shi, inferred -chi. Despite contact with Spanish the system in Huaytlas remains robust even in bilungual speech. Comparing a bilingual speaker's retelling of the story of the Achiquee against 1970s Ancash recordings, I follow Lilliana Sanchez (2010) to argue that the evidential systems scope over the focus of the sentence (or point of highest information) in Bilingual and monolingual speech.

LCUGA 2025 · University of Georgia

Without evidential

Achiquee unay-n tiempos ka-shqan.
‘The Achiquee existed in old times.’

With evidential

…chunki-sh … ka-n-mi.
‘…ten times, they say… it is (I saw).’

The reportative -sh and direct -mi appear in one telling and not the other.

Chechen · Nakh-Daghestanian

Dynamic verbs and scalar change

Through Beaver's (2017) analysis of scalar change, I examine the way in which Vendler's aspectual classes strain against verbs that encode a scale — cool, warm which are telic and widen,which is atelic. I adapt Rappaport Hovav's (2008) diagnostics for Chechen, testing telicity with words like (mela 'warm' + della 'become'), and test them in grammaticality judgments with a native speaker from Grozny.

Tarifit Berber · Morocco

Rising intonation in polar and wh-questions

Tarifit can mark a yes/no question with the particle [ma] — or drop the particle and rely on a rising tone. Field elicitation and spectrographic analysis of both question types, testing obligatory particle marking in a variety of question formation settings. Co-authored fieldwork.

Field methods project · with J. McLendon, M. McNamara, C. Augustine

Teaching

I taught Spanish at the University of Georgia for two years during my MA and for one semester after graduating — instructor of record for SPAN 1001 and 1002.

My approach is task-oriented and communication-focused, in line with ACTFL standards: grammar gets introduced in service of something students are trying to say, and class time goes to using it rather than hearing about it.

The lesson at right is a full 50-minute class on por vs. para and the demonstratives. The demonstrative sequence teaches the three-way deictic contrast (este / ese / aquel) by putting students themselves at different distances in the room — deixis is hard to explain and easy to point at.
